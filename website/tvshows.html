My Kind of TV Shows

This page showcases my favorite TV shows. Each category features embedded clips, highlighting captivating moments, iconic scenes, and compelling storytelling that make these shows unforgettable.

Sitcom K-Drama K-Variety

Sitcom

Friends

Producer: David Crane, Marta Kauffman

Year: 1994-2004

This hit sitcom follows the merry misadventures of six 20-something friends as they navigate the pitfalls of work, life and love in Manhattan, New York.

The Office (US)

Producer: Greg Daniels

Year: 2005-2013

A mockumentary on a group of typical office workers, where the workday consists of ego clashes, inappropriate behavior, tedium and romance.

How I Met Your Mother

Producer: Carter Bays, Craig Thomas

Year: 2005-2014

A sitcom following Ted Mosby and his friends in New York City, as he recounts the story of how he met his future wife to his children, narrated by a future Ted.

Modern Family

Producer: Christopher Lloyd, Steven Levitan

Year: 2009-2020

A sitcom following the hilarious and heartfelt dynamics of a blended, multi-generational family in Los Angeles.

Brooklyn Nine-Nine

Producer: Dan Goor, Michael Schur

Year: 2013-2021

Easygoing Brooklyn police detective Jake Peralta must learn to follow the rules and be a team player when his squad gets an exacting new captain.

Schitt's Creek

Producer: Daniel Levy, Eugene Levy

Year: 2015-2020

Suddenly broke, the formerly filthy-rich Rose family is reduced to living in a ramshackle motel in a town they once bought as a joke: Schitt's Creek.

K-Drama

Reply 1988

Director: Shin Won-ho

Year: 2015

Follows the lives of 5 families living on the same street in a neighbourhood called Ssangmundong in Seoul. A nostalgic look back at the year 1988.

Goblin

Director: Lee Eung-bok

Year: 2016

In his quest for a bride to break his immortal curse, Dokkaebi, a 939-year-old guardian of souls, meets a grim reaper and a sprightly student with a tragic past.

Moon Lovers

Director: Kim Kyu-tae

Year: 2016

During a total solar eclipse, 25-year-old Go Ha-jin travels back in time to the Goryeo Dynasty as Hae-soo, where she falls in love with the 4th Prince, Wang So.

Crash Landing On You

Director: Lee Jung-hyo

Year: 2019

A South Korean heiress crash-lands in North Korea while paragliding and finds herself hidden and protected by an army officer, who falls in love with her.

Hospital Playlist

Director: Shin Won-ho

Year: 2020

A medical slice-of-life drama following the lives of five doctors in their forties working at Yulje Medical Center, who have been friends since medical school.

My Liberation Notes

Director: Park Hae-yeong

Year: 2022

Follows the story of three siblings, exhausted by the monotony of day-to-day adulthood, seek to find fulfillment and freedom from their unremarkable lives.

K-Variety

Running Man

Producer: Nam Seung-yong

Year: 2010-present

Running Man is a South Korean reality-variety show where the members and guests must complete missions at various places to win the race and avoid penalty.

The Return of Superman

Producer: Jo Seong-suk

Year: 2013-present

This popular Korean reality-variety show challenges celebrity dads to take care of their kids alone for 48 hours while their wives get a much-needed break.

Knowing Bros

Producer: Jo Seung-wook

Year: 2015-present

Male celebs play make-believe as high schoolers, welcoming star transfer students every week and engaging in battles of witty humor and slapstick.

New Journey to the West

Producer: Na Young-seok

Year: 2015-2020

Each cast member takes on the role of a specific character and embarks on trips, facing challenges and completing missions through games and tasks.

Amazing Saturday

Producer: Lee Tae-kyung

Year: 2018-present

Members and guests need to perfectly finish lyrics quizzes from multiple generations to taste the foods from famous Korean traditional markets.

The Great Escape

Producer: Jung Jong-yeon

Year: 2018-2021

The show features big themed escape rooms where members must find clues and solve puzzles to escape. Each escape challenge has a different theme.
